# Acme E-commerce — VIP Customer Handling Guide
**Version:** 1.5  
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2026  
**Owner:** Sarah Chen, VP Customer Success  

---

## VIP Account Criteria

An account is classified as VIP when it meets one or more of the following thresholds:

- **ARR ≥ $100,000** — automatically flagged in the CRM
- **Strategic partner status** — designated by VP of Sales
- **Executive sponsor relationship** — any account where a C-level at Acme has a direct relationship with the customer's decision-maker

VIP status is reviewed quarterly. Accounts that fall below $80K ARR after previously qualifying are subject to a 90-day grace period before reclassification.

---

## Support Protocols for VIP Accounts

### First Response SLA
VIP accounts receive a **1-hour first response SLA** at all times, including weekends. The on-call Account Manager is responsible for this SLA for after-hours contacts.

### Dedicated Account Manager
Every VIP account is assigned a primary Account Manager (currently Roberto Leal or Amy Huang) and a backup. The backup is responsible for coverage during vacation, sick leave, or any unavailability.

### Issue Routing
All support requests from VIP accounts — regardless of issue type or severity — must be routed to the assigned Account Manager or Tier 2 Senior Agent first. Tier 1 agents are not authorized to close a VIP ticket without Account Manager acknowledgment.

### Escalation
VIP escalations that are unresolved after 4 hours go to Sarah Chen (VP Customer Success) directly. After 8 hours, the VP is expected to be on a live call with the customer.

---

## Quarterly Business Reviews (QBRs)

All VIP accounts with ARR ≥ $250K receive a mandatory QBR each quarter. Accounts between $100K and $250K receive QBRs semi-annually.

QBR preparation checklist (Account Manager responsibility):
- [ ] Usage analytics report (prior 90 days)
- [ ] Open support ticket summary
- [ ] Feature adoption metrics
- [ ] Renewal status and any at-risk signals
- [ ] Proposed agenda shared with customer 5 business days in advance

---

## Compensation Authority for VIP Accounts

Account Managers are authorized to offer the following without additional approval:
- Up to $500 in service credits
- One month free on the next billing cycle
- Waiver of overage fees for a single billing period

Compensation beyond these limits requires VP approval from Sarah Chen.

---

## CRM Hygiene

All VIP interactions must be logged in Salesforce within 24 hours, including calls, emails, and significant Slack exchanges. Failure to log interactions is tracked in the monthly Account Manager performance review.

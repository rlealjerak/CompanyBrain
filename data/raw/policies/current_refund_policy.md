# Acme E-commerce — Refund and Return Policy
**Version:** 3.0  
**Effective Date:** April 1, 2026  
**Owner:** Head of Operations, Sandra Mitchell  
**Last Reviewed:** March 28, 2026  

---

## Overview

This document supersedes all prior refund policy versions, including Version 2.1 (December 2025). Any agent, ticket, or knowledge base article referencing a 14-day return window is citing an outdated policy. The authoritative return window is **30 calendar days** from the date of delivery.

---

## Standard Return Window

Customers may request a refund or exchange within **30 calendar days** of the confirmed delivery date. No receipt is required for orders under $500 — the order confirmation email serves as proof of purchase. For orders $500 and above, original receipt or invoice number is required.

After 30 days, refunds are at the discretion of the assigned Account Manager for enterprise accounts, or a Tier 2 Support Agent for standard accounts.

---

## Eligible Items

The following items are eligible for full refund:

- Physical products in original, unopened packaging
- Defective or damaged products (photos required)
- Products not as described on the product listing
- Orders that arrived more than 5 business days past the promised delivery date

The following items are **not eligible** for standard refund:

- Digital licenses once activated
- Custom-manufactured or personalized items
- Hazardous materials
- Products marked as final sale at time of purchase

---

## Refund Process

1. Customer submits refund request via the customer portal or contacts support.
2. Tier 1 Support Agent verifies order within the 30-day window and checks eligibility.
3. For eligible claims: issue refund confirmation immediately. Refund posts to original payment method within 5–7 business days.
4. For orders over $1,000: Tier 2 approval is required before processing. SLA is 1 business day.
5. For enterprise accounts (>$100K ARR): route to the assigned Account Manager for any refund over $500.

---

## Exceptions and Escalations

Support agents are authorized to extend the return window by up to 14 additional days (total 44 days) for documented extenuating circumstances — natural disasters, medical emergencies, or proven shipping carrier failures. Any extension beyond 44 days requires manager approval from the Head of Operations.
